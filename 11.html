Rose


A rose is one of the most admired flowering plants in the world, known for its layered petals, delicate fragrance, and symbolic meaning. Belonging to the genus Rosa, roses grow as shrubs or climbing vines and come in a wide variety of colors such as red, pink, white, yellow, and even deep purple. They have been cultivated for thousands of years and are often associated with love, beauty, and passion. Roses are commonly used in gardens, bouquets, perfumes, and celebrations, making them both ornamental and culturally significant plants across many societies.

Aside, Roses also hold deep symbolic value in literature and history. For example, in the novel Romeo and Juliet by William Shakespeare, the famous line “A rose by any other name would smell as sweet” highlights the idea that names do not change the true nature of things. Additionally, the rose is a national symbol of countries such as England and United States, representing heritage, unity, and pride
