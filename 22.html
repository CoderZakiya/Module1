Sunflower

The sunflower is a tall, bright, and cheerful plant known for its large yellow petals and edible seeds. It is famous for turning its head to follow the sun across the sky, a phenomenon called heliotropism. Sunflowers are grown for their beauty, as well as for producing sunflower oil, which is widely used in cooking. They are also a source of healthy snacks and are often used in gardens to attract bees and other pollinators.

Characteristics of Sunflower

  • Tall, sturdy stem with rough leaves
  • Large, round flower head with bright yellow petals
  • Seeds are found in the center and are edible
  • Exhibits heliotropism (turning toward the sun)
  • Grows best in full sunlight and well-drained soil

History

  • Sunflowers are native to North America
  • Domesticated around 1000 B.C. by Native Americans
  • Used for food, oil, and decorative purposes
