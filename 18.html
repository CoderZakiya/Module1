Global Warming

🌍🌱Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth’s average temperature due to the buildup of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. These gases trap heat from the sun and prevent it from escaping back into space, which gradually raises the planet's temperature. As a result, global warming leads to climate change, melting glaciers, rising sea levels, extreme weather events, and disruptions to ecosystems. It is one of the most serious environmental challenges facing the world today and requires global cooperation to manage and reduce its impacts

Global warming is the slow increase in the Earth’s temperature caused mainly by greenhouse gases. Human activities such as burning fossil fuels, cutting down trees, and industrial pollution are major causes of this problem. It leads to climate change, melting glaciers, and rising sea levels. To prevent global warming, people should plant more trees, reduce pollution, and use renewable energy. Protecting the environment is essential for a safe and healthy future.

Causes of Global Warming

One of the main causes of global warming is the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas for electricity, transportation, and industry. These activities release large amounts of carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. Another major cause is deforestation. Trees absorb carbon dioxide, but when forests are cut down, this gas remains in the air and increases global temperatures. Industrial pollution and excessive use of vehicles also contribute to the problem. Rapid urbanization and population growth further increase energy use and environmental damage.

🌱Prevention of Global Warming

Global warming can be reduced by taking several important steps. First, people should reduce the use of fossil fuels and shift to renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power. Planting more trees and protecting forests can also help absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. Using public transport, saving electricity, and reducing waste are simple actions that individuals can take. Governments should enforce environmental laws and encourage clean energy technologies. Through collective effort and awareness, it is possible to slow down global warming and create a healthier environment for the future.
